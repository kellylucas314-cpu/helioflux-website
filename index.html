The Problem

We're finding cancer
too late.

By the time a CT scan or MRI finds cancer, you already have roughly one billion cells — 30 cell divisions — growing inside you. That's a tumor you can feel. Something that may have already spread.

HelioFlux detects at 5–10 million cells. Something you can't feel yet. Something that hasn't spread. Something that's still simple to treat.

The difference between detecting at one billion cells and ten million cells isn't a better test — it's a different outcome.

The Science

Every living cell
emits light.

Ultra-weak photon emissions (UPEs) are a direct, real-time signal of cellular metabolism — thousands of times fainter than a firefly, detectable only with instruments as sensitive as those in astronomical observatories. When metabolism changes, the light changes too. We built the detector to hear the difference.

Our technology doesn't shine light into you — it records the light already coming out.

Photomultiplier tube (PMT) technology — the same sensitivity used in particle physics experiments, now applied to human biology.

1

Non-Invasive Recording

Our device records the photons your cells naturally emit. No sample. No preparation. No radiation. You simply place the affected area near the detector.

2

Ultra-Sensitive Detection

Photomultiplier tubes detect light at the single-photon level. The same technology used in deep-space observation, miniaturized for biological measurement.

3

Pattern Analysis

Advanced signal processing identifies wavelength-specific signatures that distinguish healthy from malignant tissue — with 90–92% discriminant accuracy.
