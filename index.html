The Science

What are biophotons?

Every living cell emits an ultra-faint glow of light — thousands of times weaker than a firefly. These Ultra-weak Photon Emissions (UPEs) are a direct, real-time signal of cellular metabolism. When metabolism changes, the light changes too. This phenomenon was discovered over 100 years ago, but only now do we have detectors sensitive enough to measure it reliably.

The key innovation

Our technology doesn't shine light into you — it records the light coming out of you. The same light your cells have always been emitting. We just built the instrument to hear the difference between healthy and not.

1

Non-Invasive Recording

Our devices simply record the photons your cells naturally emit. No radiation, no blood draw, no tissue sample.

2

Ultra-Sensitive Detection

Using photomultiplier tubes similar to those found in astronomical observatories — detecting light at the single-photon level.

3

AI-Powered Analysis

Advanced signal processing analyzes millions of data points, identifying patterns invisible to conventional screening.

4

Actionable Insights

Results indicate detected patterns for early intervention — at a stage when outcomes are dramatically better.
