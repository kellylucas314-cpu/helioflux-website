The Problem

We are not losing the war on cancer.
We are losing the timing.

When cancer is found at Stage I, survival rates exceed 90%. When found at Stage IV, survival drops below 15%. The biology is the same. The difference is entirely when we look. Current detection requires approximately one billion cancer cells before a tumor appears on any scan. By then, the window for easy intervention has often closed.

~1B

Cancer cells required for detection by conventional imaging and most blood tests today.

5–10M

Cancer cells detectable by HelioFlux — the earliest non-invasive detection threshold in existence.

The Technology

Light the body already speaks.
We learned to listen.

Every living cell emits ultra-weak photon emissions — biophotons — a form of light produced as a natural byproduct of metabolic activity. Cancer disrupts this signal in measurable, reproducible ways. HelioFlux's platform detects and interprets that disruption.

Ultra-Weak Photon Detection

All living cells produce biophotons — photons of light invisible to the human eye but measurable with precision photomultiplier technology. HelioFlux captures and analyzes this signal at single-photon sensitivity.

Metabolic Signal Analysis

Cancer cells have fundamentally altered metabolic profiles. Their photon emission patterns differ from healthy tissue in measurable ways — in intensity, coherence, and frequency distribution — creating a reliable biomarker for early detection.

Non-Invasive Platform

No blood draw. No biopsy. No ionizing radiation. The HelioFlux measurement protocol is entirely non-invasive, making it suitable for routine screening at a scale and frequency that current modalities cannot match.
